Canadian Cities Guide¶

Major Urban Centers¶

Canada’s largest cities serve as economic, cultural, and political hubs. This chapter explores Canada’s major metropolitan areas.

Toronto, Ontario¶

  • Population: ~2.9 million (metropolitan area)

  • Founded: 1793

  • Notable Landmarks: CN Tower, Royal Ontario Museum, St. Lawrence Market

  • Culture: Multicultural hub, diverse neighborhoods

  • Economy: Finance, technology, entertainment

Vancouver, British Columbia¶

  • Population: ~0.6 million (city), ~2.6 million (metropolitan area)

  • Founded: 1886

  • Notable Landmarks: Stanley Park, Granville Island, Capilano Suspension Bridge

  • Culture: Pacific gateway, outdoor recreation

  • Economy: Port, technology, film industry

Montreal, Quebec¶

  • Population: ~1.7 million (city), ~4.3 million (metropolitan area)

  • Founded: 1642

  • Notable Landmarks: Notre-Dame Basilica, Mount Royal, Old Montreal

  • Culture: French-Canadian heritage, arts and culture

  • Economy: Aerospace, pharmaceuticals, technology

Calgary, Alberta¶

  • Population: ~1.3 million (metropolitan area)

  • Founded: 1875

  • Notable Landmarks: Calgary Tower, Stampede Park, Heritage Park

  • Culture: Western heritage, energy sector

  • Economy: Oil and gas, technology, agriculture

Ottawa, Ontario¶

  • Population: ~1.0 million (metropolitan area)

  • Founded: 1826

  • Notable Landmarks: Parliament Hill, National Gallery, Rideau Canal

  • Culture: Political center, national institutions

  • Economy: Government, technology, tourism

Winnipeg, Manitoba¶

  • Population: ~0.8 million (metropolitan area)

  • Founded: 1738

  • Notable Landmarks: The Forks, Canadian Museum for Human Rights

  • Culture: Prairie culture, arts scene

  • Economy: Transportation hub, agriculture

Quebec City, Quebec¶

  • Population: ~0.5 million (city), ~0.8 million (metropolitan area)

  • Founded: 1608

  • Notable Landmarks: Montmorency Falls, Old Quebec, Château Frontenac

  • Culture: Historic French-Canadian city, UNESCO World Heritage Site

  • Economy: Tourism, government, education

Halifax, Nova Scotia¶

  • Population: ~0.4 million (metropolitan area)

  • Founded: 1749

  • Notable Landmarks: Peggy’s Cove, Citadel, Waterfront

  • Culture: Maritime heritage, Atlantic gateway

  • Economy: Port, military, tourism
